How Consultancies Use LinkedIn to Signal Strategy

McKinsey, BCG, and EY are all-in on AI—but what’s left behind?

By Klara Rehm

As trusted advisors to the world’s most powerful corporations, consultancies try to stay ahead of the curve. One under-examined window into their evolving priorities is their public messaging—particularly on LinkedIn.

Having worked at McKinsey, I witnessed firsthand how quickly a firm’s strategic emphasis can shift. My own entry into consulting was driven by the promise of shaping ambitious climate strategies. But that idealism ran headlong into a new reality: AI fever and cost-cutting overtook climate as boardroom obsessions.

In 2024, the Guardian reported that McKinsey knew as early as 2021 that its clients were driving rising global emissions. Yet senior leadership hesitated to act—sustainability became expendable in a tougher market.

Are the firm’s public signals aligned with these internal shifts? To explore this, I analyzed the LinkedIn output of McKinsey, BCG, and EY from May to early August 2025. Each post was tagged by theme—from AI and geopolitics to ESG and HR.

AI dominates. All three firms tout their tech credentials through reports, case studies, and employee profiles. With software companies expanding into implementation services, consultancies appear eager to guard their turf.

McKinsey posts the most—and leans hardest into AI. That may be a response to media projections that BCG could surpass it within two years due to stronger tech offerings. The firm’s climate and ESG messaging, once central, has thinned noticeably.

BCG, too, emphasizes AI but strikes more balance—maintaining focus on strategy and corporate transformation. Its content suggests an attempt to remain both innovative and grounded.

EY takes a steadier approach. While tech is visible, its dominant themes remain business fundamentals. This positioning may signal durability over trend-chasing—leaving more space for long-term goals, including ESG.

In a crowded market, consultancies are not just selling services—they’re signaling relevance. LinkedIn has become their stage. What they choose to emphasize speaks volumes about what they believe clients—and recruits—want to hear.
